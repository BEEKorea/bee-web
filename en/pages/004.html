BEE has many distinctives. Some of them are as the following:

First,

Learn and Teach at the Same Time

The students of BEE need to teach others while they learn at the same time. They can study effectively when they learn and teach simultaneously.

Second,

Train the Right People

BEE provides advanced training not only for the young who are called for the ministry of the Lord but also for the mature adults spiritually who are involved in ministry to improve their pastoring and ministry. Many of them cannot take advanced training because of their ministry or family.

Third,

Teaching Supported by Field Education

The students pursue their ministry in the field while they are learning. The local churches help the students learn ministry skill and live with God.

Fourth,

Role-model of Evangelism and Discipleship

The facilitators of BEE become role models to the students when they are teaching them by being involved in evangelism and discipleship, the character of God, and pastoring skills so that the students may follow their examples.

Fifth,

Learning in the Ministry Field.

BEE trains the students where they are working in which there is no cultural or ethnic difference. It also helps them continue their ministry.

Sixth,

Efficient Teaching Method

The teaching method adopted by BEE can train more students than the method used by traditional seminaries. Traditional seminaries can train only a few among thousands of new church leaders the church needs now.

Seventh,

Flexibility

The curriculum of BEE is flexible to various ministry circumstances.

Eighth,

Cost-effective Ministry

The ministry of BEE is cost-effective compare to traditional seminaries with buildings, faculties and admin staff. Because BEE also aims at indigenized BEE ministry BEE does not support the students financially. Hence BEE ministry requires far less finance than traditional seminaries.
