Overview

This course is a sequel of Evangelism and Discipleship 1 and emphasizes discipleship must follow evangelism. You will learn how to make disciples using One-to-One Discipleship Training based on the seminar about the importance of discipleship in fulfilling the Great Commission and workshop to practice it.

  • It takes seven 150-minute sessions (total 17.5 hours) to complete the course.

Contents

  1. Understand disciple making is crucial in the Great Commission and examine yourself as a disciple maker.
  2. Acknowledge the importance of regular fellowship through the Bible with the Lord.
  3. Study the content of One-to-One Discipleship Training in depth.
  4. Learn the benefits the church will get when the church is involved in disciple making and how to invite believers to it.
  5. Study the multiplication principle and its benefits, and the principles in disciple making.

Prerequisite

  • Galatians, Christian Life

Overview

This course is designed as a sequel to One-to-One Discipleship Training. You will learn that Jesus Christ in the only Lord we must follow as the subtitle, "Lessons of Disciples from the Gospel of Mark." You will pursue a higher level of discipleship studying what Jesus Christ taught to his first disciples when he was in the world and how they responded to him.

  • It takes seven 150-minute sessions (total 17.5 hours) to complete the course.

Contents

  1. Gain a deeper intuition about Jesus Christ by finding the answer to the question, "Who is Jesus?" and realize who Jesus is and what kind of disciples we must be and for what we are called.
  2. Challenge you to be a disciple Jesus intended and follow him even to his suffering by finding the answer to the question, “What kind of Messiah is he, and what does it mean to follow him?”

Prerequisite

  • None

Overview

This course consists of 12 lessons and present Christian life as a journey starting from the moment of spiritual birth in Christ toward maturity. You will find the goal of this journey, God's and our roles from the Bible. Also you will study about your inner maturity and its application to your life and your ministries toward believers and non-believers. This course deal with the topics of One-to-One Discipleship Training in depth which provides the biblical background of the course.

  • It takes twelve 150-minute sessions (total 30 hours) to complete the course.

Contents

  1. Define the goal and the process for achieving spiritual growth, and spiritual maturity, and explain the three major principles that influence it.
  2. Identify fourteen godly characteristics that will be evident as a result of spiritual growth, and apply them to your life.
  3. Explain how each of the following contributes to spiritual growth: Bible study, prayer, and faith.
  4. Create a list of life's goals, arrange those goals in order of priority and develop a plan of action for implementing those activities.
  5. Define the five reasons for believers' sufferings.
  6. Identify the two fundamental ministry responsibilities all believers have for one another, and the two fundamental ministries that all believers have to non-believers.
  7. Describe the six pieces of the believer's “armor of God,” their spiritual significance, and their practical value.

Prerequisite

  • Galatians, Romans

Overview

Contents
